My role at Journey Further is quite different to that of a typical Front End Developer. I work within the Conversion Rate Optimisation (CRO) team, which runs testing on client websites to ascertain which version customers prefer. My role is to build the tests, primarily using Javascript, jQuery, HTML and CSS, but I also have a number of other responsibilities including:

Working with Clients and Specialists

I work face-to-face with both clients and Conversion Specialists to understand client needs, and how particular experiments might help achieve those. This might include discussing particular goals and breaking those down into simple changes that can be made to give us vital customer insights, or working with specialists to decide the best way to approach a particular problem.

Adapting quickly to changing client needs

As I work with a number of clients it is important that I am comfortable using whatever system that client uses, which means that I am used to quickly getting up to speed with new technologies as needs change.

Quality Assurance

I am also responsible for making sure that any tests built meet our high standards, which requires intense attention to detail to make sure that there are no problems caused anywhere on the website, and also an ability to keep track of multiple projects at the same time to ensure that tests don’t conflict with each other.

Improving Internal Systems

The side of my job that I enjoy the most is working on our internal team processes, and ensuring that our team works as efficiently as possible. One example of this was my idea to move to a sprint based development cycle, which significantly reduced the amount of time spent on admin and allowed more of our time to be spent on client work. This has made a significant difference in the number of tests that we are able to produce.
